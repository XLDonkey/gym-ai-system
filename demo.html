How it works

From camera to counted rep

1

Camera captures the person

Phone or tablet pointed at the machine. Works on any modern browser — Chrome recommended.

2

MoveNet detects 17 keypoints

Google's MoveNet Lightning model identifies joints in real time at 30fps — shoulders, elbows, wrists, hips, knees.

3

Elbow angle drives rep detection

Shoulder → Elbow → Wrist angle is tracked frame by frame. A rep is counted when the angle cycles from extended to contracted and back.

4

Neural network classifies the rep

A 5→12→8→3 MLP trained on 34 recorded sequences scores each rep: Good, Bad, or False Movement.
