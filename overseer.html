Section 01

Vision & End Goal

The Overseer is the invisible intelligence that runs beneath every machine — tracking who is training, how hard, and how well.

The Overseer Experience — Start to Finish

1

🚪 Member enters gym

NFC card tap registers arrival in GymMaster. Face ID camera at the entrance confirms identity. Session begins automatically — no app required.

2

🏋️ Member sits at machine

Occupancy camera detects a person at the station. The rep-counting camera begins pose estimation immediately. Weight stack camera reads the plate configuration.

3

🤖 AI does the work

MoveNet tracks joint angles in real time. LSTM model classifies each rep as good or bad. Total weight lifted is calculated from plate colours. Form score is computed per set.

4

📊 Session record created

AI Overseer combines: Who (face ID) + How Many (rep count) + How Much (weight) + How Well (form score). One structured event sent to Supabase.

5

🏆 Leaderboard & profile updated automatically

Tablet displays refresh in real time. Member profile updated. Leaderboard re-ranked. Zero manual entry. Zero friction.

Before — The Honesty System

  • Members self-report reps and weights
  • Data is inconsistent or simply not collected
  • No form feedback — injuries go undetected
  • Leaderboards require manual effort to maintain
  • No visibility into machine utilisation
  • Generic training plans — no personalisation

After — AI Verified

  • Every rep counted and classified by AI
  • Weight read automatically from coloured plates
  • Form score on every set — coaching at scale
  • Leaderboard updates in real time, automatically
  • Full machine utilisation heatmaps
  • Personalised insights based on real data
🔒

Member Retention

Members who see real progress data stay longer. Gamification (leaderboards, PRs, streaks) driven by verified data creates genuine habit loops and community.

💎

Premium Positioning

No other gym in Australia does this at scale. The AI Overseer becomes a core differentiator — justifying premium membership pricing and media attention.

📦

Licensing Opportunity

Once proven at XL Fitness, the system can be licensed to other gyms globally. A SaaS product built on top of a working proof-of-concept in a real gym.

The Data Flywheel

Every card tap at a machine generates a ground-truth label: we know exactly who sat at that machine and when. This ties directly to the face ID model — each tap becomes a training sample, teaching the system to recognise that member without the tap. As the model improves, fewer taps are needed to maintain confidence. The flywheel compounds: more members → more data → better models → fewer taps needed → better member experience → more members. The system becomes more capable the more it's used, without any manual intervention.

XL Fitness — Venue-Specific Notes

🏗
Ceiling: 3.2–3.5m suspended T-bar black acoustic tiles. Mount Camera 1 (dome) using threaded rod anchored to structural concrete slab above tiles — same method used for existing decorative halo lights.
🪟
Critical — Windows: Floor-to-ceiling windows on multiple walls create severe backlighting. Camera 2 (rep counter) MUST be mounted on the structural purple columns facing INWARD, never toward windows.
⚡
Regulatory: All data cabling must be installed by an ACMA-registered cabler in Australia. Get qualified tradespeople for camera cabling — not general electricians.

Section 09

Data Strategy

The model is only as good as the data. Film everything. Label everything. Ship faster.

Training Data Approach

1.
Film real gym sessions
Long, uninterrupted takes. Multiple people. Multiple rep ranges. Controlled lighting.
2.
Label using the Labeler tool
Section-based labelling — not individual reps. Drag ranges. Export JSON. Send to Donkey.
3.
Unlock LSTM at 500+ sequences
Currently at 445. Need ~55 more. Each 10-minute filming session yields many sequences.
4.
Data flywheel takes over
Card tap → face ID label → automatic retraining. System improves hands-free.

6 Label Types (State Machine)

good_rep

Full range of motion, controlled movement, meets quality threshold

bad_rep

Partial ROM, momentum used, poor form detected by joint angles

false_movement

Non-rep motion — repositioning, fidgeting, phone use

user_seated_engaged

Person at machine, ready to exercise, between sets

user_present_not_engaged

Person present but not using machine (resting, talking)

no_user_present

Machine empty. Resets the session tracking state.
