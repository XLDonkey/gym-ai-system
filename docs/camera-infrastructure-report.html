⚠ Critical Insight — The Window Problem

XL Fitness has floor-to-ceiling glazing on multiple walls. Any camera aimed toward a window during daylight will be severely backlit — the user will appear as a silhouette. The solution: mount cameras on or near the window wall, shooting inward. The light becomes your friend instead of your enemy. For machines in the centre of the floor, use WDR cameras rated at 120dB+.

Given the window backlight problem, Camera 2 (rep counter) should be mounted on or near the window wall, shooting inward across the machine. 4. Mounting Strategy — XL Fitness Specific

Using the Structural Columns (Best Option)

The purple structural columns at the window wall junctions are the ideal mount points for Camera 2. They are:

  • Solid structural concrete/steel — will hold any camera bracket
  • Already have fixings (exit signs, signage)
  • Positioned at the window wall — cameras shoot inward, eliminating backlight
  • Corner positioning gives diagonal view across multiple machines
  • 300–400mm width accommodates standard camera brackets

Ceiling Mounting for Camera 1

The suspended T-bar ceiling tiles cannot support camera weight. Three options:

  1. Tile replacement plates — swap a 600×600mm tile for a steel plate with camera mount. Standard installation method. Electrician anchors to the structural slab above.
  2. Span brackets — a bracket that bridges two T-bar rails, distributing load. No ceiling tile removed. Camera hangs between tiles.
  3. Threaded rod drop mounts — anchor to structural slab above, drop threaded rod to camera mount. Most secure, invisible from below. Matches the existing halo light installation method.

Recommendation: Option 3 — same method used for the decorative halo lights. Clean, matches the existing aesthetic, electrician already familiar with the plenum space.

Cable Routing

  • All cables run through the plenum (above suspended ceiling tiles) — fully hidden
  • Black CAT6A cable — matches ceiling aesthetic for any exposed runs
  • Each camera needs ONE cable — PoE carries both power and data
  • Route to a central point in each zone where a PoE switch can be located
  • PoE switches can sit in the plenum space or in a small comms cabinet
🎨 Aesthetic Consideration

XL Fitness is a premium, design-led space. Camera housings should be black mini-domes or black bullet cameras — these virtually disappear against the black ceiling and dark walls. Avoid white or grey cameras. All cabling must be fully concealed in the plenum. The goal: members should barely notice the cameras are there.

5. Network Infrastructure

How PoE Works

Power over Ethernet (PoE) means a single CAT6 cable to each camera carries both data AND power. No separate power cable needed. This dramatically simplifies installation — one trade (IT/data cabling) installs everything.

Camera Placement for Lat Pulldown

Ideal position for Camera 2 (rep counter):

• Mount on the structural purple column nearest the lat pulldown machine
• Height: 1.2–1.5m (shoulder height of a seated user)
• Angle: 90° side-on to the machine — looking directly at the user's profile
• Distance: 2–3m from the user
• Camera should be facing inward/away from the windows — use the window light behind the camera to illuminate the user

What this gives us:
• Clean elbow angle tracking — side profile is optimal for MoveNet
• Good lighting — window behind camera illuminates the user naturally
• No backlight issues
• Full body visible from head to hips
🚫 Do NOT do this

Do not mount the camera on the same side as the windows with the camera aimed toward the windows. The user will be a silhouette and MoveNet will fail to detect keypoints reliably. Always position Camera 2 so the windows are behind the camera.
