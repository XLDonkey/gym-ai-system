Section 01

The Mocap-Style Rig Concept

Rather than drilling cameras into walls or punching through acoustic tiles, XL Fitness will install a professional overhead rail grid — the same approach used on VR motion-capture stages, Amazon Go store ceilings, and premium retail fit-outs. Cameras mount on adjustable drop arms from a black Unistrut rail matrix, giving complete X/Y/Z repositionability without touching the existing ceiling structure.

Repositioning a Camera

  • Loosen M8 clamp bolt with Allen key
  • Slide arm to new position along rail
  • Re-tighten bolt — takes ~60 seconds
  • Loosen ball head, re-aim camera, re-lock
  • No new cabling required (cable loops in rail)

Section 06

Mounting to Existing Ceiling

ℹ
Ceiling type: 3.2–3.5m suspended T-bar black acoustic tile grid

Standard commercial suspended ceiling with steel T-bar frame holding acoustic tiles. The structural plenum above contains HVAC, existing light fitting supports, and steel purlins/beams. Three mounting options are available — Option A is strongly preferred.

A
T-Bar Grid Clamp — No Drilling ✓ PREFERRED
Standard T-bar suspension clips attach directly to the existing steel T-bar grid. Zero damage to tiles. Zero drilling into the structural slab. Same principle as track lighting used in galleries and retail stores.
  • Clamp type: Unistrut B3106 T-bar clamp or equivalent
  • Load rating: 15kg per clamp — Unistrut rail + 3 cameras weighs ~3kg total
  • Requires: Minimum 2 clamps per rail segment (4 for longer spans)
  • No tiles disturbed. No trades beyond rigging required.
~$8–12 per clamp No structural engineer required Fully reversible Zero ceiling damage
B
Threaded Rod Hangers — Plenum Structure
If the T-bar grid can't carry the full rig load, threaded M10 rods hang from structural elements in the plenum: HVAC brackets, steel purlins, or existing light fitting frames. Pop a tile aside, locate steel, clamp rod, replace tile.
  • Rod: M10 threaded rod, hot-dip galvanised
  • Anchor: Beam clamp onto steel purlin or HVAC support in plenum
  • Tiles: Removed and replaced — no damage if done carefully
  • Use when T-bar grid alone insufficient for span length
~$15–25 per hanger point Minimal tile disruption Higher load capacity
C
Wall-to-Wall Span — Near-Wall Zones
For zones within 2–3m of perimeter walls, rails can span wall-to-wall without any ceiling contact. Rail anchored directly into masonry walls at each end — zero ceiling involvement.
  • Wall anchor: Masonry bolt or concrete anchor (M12 min.)
  • Span: Up to 8–10m for 41×41 Unistrut depending on load
  • Requires confirmation of wall material (masonry / concrete column)
~$50–100 per wall anchor Zero ceiling involvement Perimeter zones only

Who Installs

🔩 Structural Rigging

Commercial rigging company or experienced commercial fit-out contractor. Responsible for rail grid installation and all mechanical mounting. Not electrical.

ACMA-registered data cabler — required by Australian law for any structured CAT6 cabling installation. Confirm registration before engaging.

    Print/share the Contractor Brief (Section 08). Request quotes from 2–3 commercial rigging or fit-out contractors. Ask for indicative Phase 2 + full-gym rates at same time.
  3. 3

    AI model training doesn't need the permanent rig. Use this window to build the dataset while infrastructure is being finalised.
  4. 4
    Order cameras and switches for Phase 1 rig
    3× Hikvision cameras + TP-Link PoE switch. Total hardware cost ~$550. Order early — allow 1–2 weeks delivery for Hikvision units.
  5. 5
    Schedule rigging contractor + data cabler on same day
    Coordinate both trades to attend simultaneously — rigging installs rail while cabler runs CAT6A. Saves a second site visit and reduces disruption to the gym floor.
  6. 6
    Commission Phase 1 rig — test all three cameras
    Once rail and cables are in: mount cameras, power up via PoE switch, confirm RTSP streams on Mac Mini. Aim cameras, lock ball heads. Run AI model against live feed.
  7. 7

    Based on Phase 1 learnings, refine arm lengths and camera positions for Phase 2. Engage rigging company for next zone (5 machines, ~15 cameras). Repeat at scale.
