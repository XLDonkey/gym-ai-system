Camera Types — What's Right for a Gym?

Four fundamentally different camera form factors — each with distinct strengths. Understanding the differences is critical before you buy anything.

🎯

Turret / Eyeball Camera

Adjustable ball-joint design — points in any direction within a wide range

★ XL Fitness Pick
Pros
  • Adjustable angle post-install
  • Compact & discreet
  • Low profile, sits flush
  • Excellent for column mounts
Cons
  • Not 360° — needs aiming
  • Limited adjustment range
🏋️ Recommended for:  Camera 2 (rep counter) — column mounts at XL Fitness
🔵

Fisheye 360° Camera

Single ultra-wide lens captures a full 360° hemisphere from ceiling

★ XL Fitness Pick
Pros
  • One camera covers 4–6 machines
  • Zero blind spots
  • Built-in occupancy analytics
  • Virtual PTZ in software
Cons
  • Distorted barrel edges
  • Less detail per subject
  • Harder AI pose processing
🏋️ Recommended for:  Camera 1 (zone occupancy) — ceiling mount
🔄

PTZ Camera

Pan-Tilt-Zoom — motorised camera that moves and zooms remotely

Security Only
Pros
  • Covers huge area
  • 25× optical zoom
  • AI auto-tracking
Cons
  • Expensive ($800–2,000+)
  • One direction at a time
  • Moving parts = wear & failure
  • Overkill for per-machine AI
🔒 Use for:  General gym security — NOT for AI machine tracking
📡

Bullet Camera

Cylindrical fixed-direction camera — obvious, long-range deterrent

Entrance / Corridor
Pros
  • Long range (up to 60–80m)
  • Obvious visual deterrent
  • IP67 weatherproof
Cons
  • Fixed angle — no adjust
  • Bulky & conspicuous
  • Not ideal for close-range AI
🚪 Recommended for:  Entrances, corridors, weight room aisles

Camera Placement Strategy

C1

Camera 1 — Zone Occupancy

Mount above machine zone using threaded rod drop mount anchored to structural slab. Position fisheye at ceiling height (3.2–3.5m). One unit covers 4–6 machines in 360° hemisphere.

C2

Camera 2 — Rep Counter

Mount on purple structural columns, facing INWARD (away from windows). Height: 1.2–1.5m. Windows remain behind the camera = natural front lighting of the user's body.

⚠️ CRITICAL: Never aim Camera 2 toward windows. The 130dB WDR helps but backlight will still degrade pose estimation. Column placement naturally solves this.
C3

Camera 3 — Weight Stack

Fixed mount on machine frame or adjacent wall, aimed tight at the weight selector pin area. Distance: 0.5–1m. Use 4mm lens for precise framing at this close range.

Ceiling Mount Procedure (T-Bar Ceiling)

  1. Pop ceiling tile aside → locate structural slab above (concrete or steel deck)
  2. Drill and rawl-anchor a M8 threaded rod hanger into structural slab
  3. Drop threaded rod to desired height (camera lens at ~3m AFF)
  4. Attach camera mounting plate to rod end
  5. Route CAT6A cable through ceiling plenum to nearest PoE switch
  6. Terminate CAT6A with RJ45 in junction box above tile
  7. Replace / trim ceiling tile around rod

🇦🇺 Australian Licensing Requirements

🔧

DIY Allowed

No license required

Camera mounting, aiming, configuration, software setup, NVR configuration. Once cabling is in place, camera physical install is DIY-friendly.

📡

Licensed Cabler Required

ACMA registration needed

All data cabling (CAT6/CAT6A) in Australia requires an ACMA-registered Open Cabling (Restricted) or Open Cabling cabler. This includes ceiling plenum runs and wall terminations.

⚡

Licensed Electrician Required

240V electrical work

Any 240V power connections for PoE switches, NAS units, or NVRs require a licensed electrician. PoE cameras themselves run on PoE (low voltage) via the data cable.

Section 7

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about gym camera installation in Australia.

Do I need planning permission to install security cameras in a gym in Australia?

▼

Generally, no planning permission is required for internal CCTV cameras in a privately owned commercial premises. However, you must comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and relevant state surveillance legislation.


Key requirements include:

  • Display clearly visible signage notifying members that CCTV is in operation
  • Have a documented CCTV Privacy Policy outlining what is recorded, how long it's stored, and who can access it
  • Cameras must not record areas where privacy is expected (e.g., change rooms, toilets)
  • AI biometric data collection (face recognition, gait analysis) may require additional consent

For AI rep counting that captures body pose data, include specific consent in your membership agreement. Consult a privacy lawyer for your specific use case.

What is the maximum camera run length on CAT6/CAT6A?

▼

The IEEE 802.3af/at PoE standard specifies a maximum run of 100 metres (328ft) of CAT6/CAT6A from switch to camera. This covers both data and power transmission.


In practice for XL Fitness:

  • Most camera runs will be 15–25m — well within limits
  • Use CAT6A (not standard CAT6) for better performance and PoE power delivery efficiency
  • If a run must exceed 90m, use a PoE extender/repeater (e.g., TP-Link TL-PoE170S) — adds another 100m
  • Never use pre-made cables for ceiling runs — always terminate bulk cable with proper RJ45 keystones

Can I do the camera installation myself?

▼

Partially. Australian law has specific requirements:

  • ✓ DIY: Camera mounting, aiming, and configuration once cabling is installed
  • ✗ Must be licensed: All data cabling (CAT6/CAT6A runs) requires an ACMA-registered Open Cabling cabler — this is a licensed trade in Australia
  • ✗ Must be licensed: Any 240V power connections for switches/NAS require a licensed electrician

Recommendation: hire a cabler to install all CAT6A runs and patch panels, then handle camera mounting, aiming, and software configuration yourself to save cost.

What internet speed do I need for remote access / cloud backup?

▼

For the XL Fitness AI Overseer system, internet speed requirements depend on your architecture:

  • Local AI processing only (Mac Mini M4 Pro edge): Minimal internet required — AI runs locally. Only remote access/dashboard needs bandwidth (~10–25 Mbps upload)
  • Cloud backup of footage: At 2MP / 30fps per camera, each camera uses ~1–2 Mbps continuously. For 15 cameras: ~20–30 Mbps sustained upload. A business fibre connection of 100 Mbps symmetric is ideal.
  • Phase 1 (1 machine): Any standard NBN business connection (25+ Mbps) is more than enough

For Phase 2 and beyond, recommend a business-grade NBN 250 or Enterprise Ethernet connection to the gym.

How long does footage need to be stored legally in Australia?

▼

There is no single national law specifying a minimum CCTV retention period for commercial gyms in Australia — it varies by state and context.


General guidance for a gym:

  • Minimum recommended: 30 days — covers most incident investigation windows
  • Industry best practice: 90 days for commercial fitness facilities
  • Insurance requirements: Your insurer may specify a minimum — check your policy
  • Incident footage: If an incident occurs, retain the relevant footage indefinitely until resolved
  • AI training data: Data used for AI model training requires explicit member consent and should be anonymised where possible

Document your retention policy in writing and review it with your legal advisor annually. The OAIC (Office of the Australian Information Commissioner) has guidelines on CCTV and privacy at oaic.gov.au.
